About Me

Md Masud Mia is a materials engineer with a Bachelor of Science in Materials and Metallurgical Engineering from Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET), where he graduated with a CGPA of 3.05/4.0. A dedicated researcher and practitioner, he excels in computational materials science, particularly Density Functional Theory (DFT) simulations, and holds expertise in nanoscale characterization techniques like nano-FTIR and TERS, alongside thin-film deposition methods such as CVD and PVD.

Md Masud Mia has led material selection and failure analysis for gas transmission pipelines at Titas Gas Transmission and Distribution PLC since January 2025, ensuring compliance with API, ASTM, and ASME standards to enhance corrosion resistance and system reliability. His undergraduate thesis utilized DFT+U to investigate Mo-doped graphene-like ZnO, revealing insights into bandgap modulation for optoelectronic applications like LEDs and solar cells. As an author of educational books on Chemistry, Physics, and BCS topics, and mentor to over 300 students, his blend of technical prowess and educational passion positions him as a key contributor to advancing energy infrastructure and nanotechnology. Currently, he continues to innovate in materials integrity management under industry guidance, eager to tackle complex challenges in 2D materials and biosensing.

Undergraduate Thesis

Dec. 2021 – May 2022

Title: Theoretical Investigation of Structural, Electronic and Optical Properties of Graphene-like ZnO with Mo Doping: A DFT+U Study

  • • Performed detailed analysis of structural and electronic properties of Mo-doped graphene-like ZnO (g-ZnO) using Density Functional Theory with Hubbard U correction (DFT+U), evaluating lattice stability, bond lengths, and formation energies to assess the impact of doping on material integrity and potential for semiconductor applications.
  • • Investigated electronic band structures, density of states (DOS), and bandgap modulation induced by Mo doping reveal mechanisms for tuning electrical conductivity and carrier mobility, which are critical for the advancement of optoelectronic devices such as LEDs and solar cells.
  • • The optical properties explored including UV absorption spectra, dielectric functions, optical conductivity, and refractive index variations demonstrating enhanced photoelectric response and potential applications in photovoltaic and sensor technologies through computational simulations.
  • • Using advanced software tools such as Quantum ESPRESSO or VASP for ab initio calculations, interpreting results to provide insights into dopant-site preferences and their effects on magnetic and optical behaviors, contributing to the theoretical foundation for novel 2D materials in nanotechnology.
